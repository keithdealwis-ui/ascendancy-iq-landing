01 — Vision

Vision

A world where a single person with the right AI workforce can build, market, and scale a business that previously required fifty people.

Not by working harder. Not by automating the edges. By compressing the entire workforce — engineering, design, marketing, operations — into an autonomous system that executes at enterprise grade, for pennies a day.

Ascendency IQ is the platform that makes that real. Built by one person, to run one person's business, then packaged for everyone else who wants the same leverage.

06 — Personas

Personas

Two primary personas with different needs, objections, and conversion paths.

MC

Marcus Chen

The Technical CTO

CTO / VP Engineering at Series A/B SaaS (20-80 people). US/UK/EU tech hubs. Skeptical. Evaluates tools like an engineer.

Cares about

Architecture integrity, security surface, auditability, integration with existing stack

What converts him

GitHub repo, real commit history, architecture docs, 30-min technical call with no slides

His objection

"This is impressive but I can't put this in front of my board without understanding the failure modes."

SO

Sandra Okafor

The Non-Technical Founder

Founder/CEO at bootstrapped or seed-stage (5-25 people). Dubai, London, Lagos, Singapore. Buys people, not products.

Cares about

Will it actually work, cost vs developer, accountability, seeing it work before committing

What converts her

Cost comparison (£60-80k vs $0.30/day), 20-min video demo, Keith's credentials, clear onboarding promise

Her objection

"I've tried automation tools before and they always need a technical person to maintain them."

Dual-Narrative Strategy

Lead with the outcome (Sandra stops scrolling). Prove it with the mechanism (Marcus keeps reading).

"My engineering team shipped 4 PRs yesterday. I sent one Telegram message."

↑ Sandra is hooked

"Here's exactly how it works: HMAC-signed task queue, auto-polling sidecar every 15s, append-only audit trail..."

↑ Marcus stays for the architecture
